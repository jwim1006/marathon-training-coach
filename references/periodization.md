# Periodization & Progressive Overload

Reference for designing training blocks, adjusting load, and understanding adaptation timelines.

## 3:1 Loading Pattern

The standard approach: **3 weeks building, 1 week recovery.**

| Week | Volume | Intensity |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | 100% | Normal |
| 2 | 105-110% | Normal |
| 3 | 110-115% | Peak for block |
| 4 (Recovery) | 60-65% | No Z4+ work |

### Recovery Week Structure
- Volume: 40-50% of peak week
- No Z4 or Z5 sessions
- Keep easy runs truly easy (Z1-Z2)
- Include strides to maintain neuromuscular activation
- Good time for massage, extra sleep, flexibility work

### Adjustments
- **Older runners (45+):** Consider 2:1 loading (2 build, 1 recovery)
- **Injury-prone:** Consider 2:1 loading
- **High foundation, returning:** Can handle 3:1 or even 4:1

## Long Run Progression

Build the long run over 12-16 weeks toward peak distance:

```
Week 1:  16km
Week 2:  18km
Week 3:  20km
Week 4:  14km (recovery week)
Week 5:  20km
Week 6:  22km
Week 7:  24km
Week 8:  16km (recovery week)
Week 9:  26km
Week 10: 28km
Week 11: 30km
Week 12: 20km (recovery week)
Week 13: 32-35km (peak)
Week 14: 25km (taper begins)
Week 15: 18km
Week 16: Race prep only
```

**Rules:**
- Never increase the long run by more than 2-3km per week
- Peak long run: 32-35km (no need to run the full marathon distance)
- Long run should be 25-30% of weekly volume (max)
- If long run is > 30% of weekly km, you need more mid-week volume

## Weekly Structure Template

| Day | Role | Example |
|-----|------|---------|
| Monday | Recovery or rest | Z1 30-45min or off |
| Tuesday | Quality session 1 | Tempo/threshold/intervals |
| Wednesday | Easy run | Z1-Z2 45-60min |
| Thursday | Quality session 2 | Intervals/hills/VO2max |
| Friday | Recovery + strides | Z1 30-45min + 6x20s strides |
| Saturday | Long run | Z2 with Z3 progression (later phases) |
| Sunday | Rest or cross-train | Off, cycling, swimming, yoga |

### Principles
- Separate hard days by 48+ hours
- Easy day after long run
- Keep the same structure week to week (consistency helps planning)
- 80% of sessions at Z1-Z2, 20% at Z3+

## Adaptation Timelines

Understanding how long adaptations take helps set realistic expectations:

| System | Time to See Changes | Time to Optimize |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Neuromuscular (coordination) | 1-2 weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| Lactate clearance | 2-4 weeks | 6-8 weeks |
| Aerobic enzymes | 3-4 weeks | 8-12 weeks |
| Mitochondrial density | 4-8 weeks | 12-16 weeks |
| Capillarization | 6-12 weeks | 16-24 weeks |
| Cardiac adaptations | 8-12 weeks | 24+ weeks |
| Bone/tendon strength | 12-24 weeks | 1-2 years |

**Key insight:** Base phases should be longer than athletes want. The deepest adaptations (capillaries, cardiac, bone/tendon) take months. There are no shortcuts.

### Why Easy Training Works (Zone 2)

| Adaptation | Timeline | Mechanism |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Increased mitochondrial density | 4-8 weeks | More cellular powerhouses for aerobic energy |
| Capillarization | 6-12 weeks | More blood vessels deliver oxygen to muscles |
| Improved fat oxidation | 4-6 weeks | Shifts fuel use from glycogen to fat |
| Increased stroke volume | 8-12 weeks | Heart pumps more blood per beat |

Zone 2 is the foundation — never sacrifice it for more intensity.

## Foundation-Based Progression Rates

| Foundation Level | Progression Rate | Rationale |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| True beginner (< 1 year) | 5-10%/week | Everything is new; high injury risk |
| Some experience (1-3 years) | 10%/week | Standard progression |
| Strong foundation, returning | 10-15%/week | Body remembers; rebuilding is faster |
| Recently fit, no break | Maintain or small build | Already adapted; focus on specificity |

**Dormant fitness:** An experienced runner returning from a break can rebuild faster than a beginner. Muscle memory, movement efficiency, and cardiovascular adaptations partially persist. Expect 4-8 weeks to return to previous form.

**However:** Injury-related breaks require more caution than life-circumstance breaks. Always validate with the athlete.

## Phase Characteristics

### Base Phase (Weeks 1-4)
- Focus: Aerobic base, gradual volume build
- Intensity: 85-90% Z1-Z2, introduce light Z3 and Z5
- Long run: Build from 90min to 2hrs
- Key: Patience. Don't rush this phase.

### Build Phase (Weeks 5-8)
- Focus: Threshold work, race simulations
- Intensity: 80% Z1-Z2, 20% Z3-Z5
- Long run: Build to 2.5hrs, add Z3 finishes
- Key: Quality over quantity in hard sessions

### Peak Phase (Weeks 9-12)
- Focus: Highest volume, race-specific intensity
- Intensity: 80% Z1-Z2, 20% Z3-Z4 (race-pace dominant)
- Long run: Peak at 2.5-3hrs with significant Z3 portions
- Key: Hardest phase. Prioritize sleep and nutrition.

### Taper Phase (Weeks 13-16)
- Focus: Volume reduction, maintain sharpness
- Volume reduction: ~20% wk13, ~40% wk14, ~60% wk15, race week minimal
- Maintain some Z3/Z4 work to stay sharp
- Key: Trust the taper. You won't lose fitness.
