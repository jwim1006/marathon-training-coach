# Race Day Execution Guide

Read this reference when a race is within 7 days. Covers pacing, nutrition, hydration, caffeine, and weather adjustments.

## Pacing Strategies

| Strategy | Best For | Execution |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Even splits | First-timers, conservative goals | Run every km at goal pace from the start |
| Slight negative | Experienced, controlled starters | First half at goal + 5-10s/km, second half at goal |
| Progressive | Very experienced, aggressive goals | First 15km easy, 15-30km at goal, 30-42km push |

**Recommended for most runners:** Slight negative split. Bank time with discipline, not speed.

### Pacing by Segment

- **0-10km:** Controlled. Resist the crowd energy. Should feel easy.
- **10-21km:** Settle into goal pace. Check in with body.
- **21-30km:** Maintain. This is where the race begins.
- **30-35km:** The wall zone. Shorten mental focus to the next km.
- **35-42km:** Draw on reserves. If you paced well, you can push here.

## Heart Rate Ceiling

| Event | Max Sustainable HR |
|-------|-------------------|
| Marathon | 85-88% of LTHR |

Exceeding this in the first half guarantees a catastrophic fade in the second half. If HR spikes above 88% LTHR before halfway, **slow down immediately**.

## Red Flags During Race

- **HR 10+ bpm above expected at same pace** — slow down NOW
- **Quad burn before halfway** — started too fast, back off
- **GI distress** — slow down, focus on hydration, skip next gel
- **Dizziness or confusion** — walk, hydrate, assess whether to continue
- **Sharp localized pain** — stop and assess, do not push through

## Pre-Race Nutrition

### Carb Loading (2-3 Days Before)
- **Target:** 8-10g carbs per kg body weight per day
- **Sources:** White rice, pasta, potatoes, bread, bananas (low fiber)
- **Avoid:** High-fiber foods, new foods, excessive fat
- **Example (70kg runner):** 560-700g carbs/day

### Race Morning (3 Hours Before Start)
- **Target:** 100-150g carbs
- **Examples:** Toast with jam, oatmeal with banana, bagel with honey
- **Rules:** Nothing new. Only foods tested in training.
- **30min before start:** Sip sports drink (20-30g carbs)

## During-Race Nutrition

### Carbohydrate Intake
- **Target:** 60-90g carbs per hour (for events > 2.5 hours)
- **Start early:** First gel at 45min mark, then every 45-60min
- **Sources:** Gels, chews, sports drink
- **Gut training is essential** — practice in training for 4-6 weeks before race

### Fueling Schedule Example (3:30 Target)

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| 45min | Gel #1 + water |
| 1:30 | Gel #2 + water |
| 2:15 | Gel #3 + water |
| 3:00 | Gel #4 (caffeinated) + water |

### Hydration

| Conditions | Fluid/Hour | Sodium/Hour |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Cool (< 18C) | 400-600ml | 300-500mg |
| Moderate (18-25C) | 600-800ml | 500-700mg |
| Hot (> 25C) | 800-1000ml | 700-1000mg |

- Drink at aid stations — don't carry unless race requires it
- Take water with every gel to aid absorption
- Don't overdrink — hyponatremia is dangerous

## Caffeine Strategy

| Protocol | Timing | Dose |
|----------|--------|------|
| Pre-race | 30-60min before start | 3-6mg/kg body weight |
| During race | Caffeinated gels in back half | 1-2mg/kg |

- **Example (70kg runner):** 200-400mg pre-race (2-4 cups coffee equivalent)
- Save caffeinated gels for after halfway — caffeine effect peaks at 30-60min
- **Caution:** Practice caffeine timing in training. Some athletes get GI distress.
- If you don't normally use caffeine, don't start on race day

## Gut Training Guidance

The GI system adapts to fueling during exercise, but only if you practice:

1. **Weeks 8-12:** Start taking gels during long runs (1 gel per long run)
2. **Weeks 4-8:** Increase to race-day fueling rate (gel every 45-60min)
3. **Weeks 1-4:** Full race nutrition rehearsal on at least 2 long runs
4. Test the **exact products** you'll use on race day

## Weather Adjustments

### Hot Weather (> 25C)
- Slow goal pace by 1-3% per 5C above 15C
- Lower HR ceiling by 5 bpm
- Increase fluid intake to 800-1000ml/hr
- Pour water on head/neck at aid stations
- Consider ice in hat or bandana

### Cold Weather (< 5C)
- Arm warmers / gloves you can discard
- Vaseline on exposed skin
- Start slightly slower (cold muscles need longer warmup)
- Fluid needs decrease but don't skip hydration

### Rain
- Anti-chafe everywhere (nipples, inner thighs, feet)
- Hat with brim to keep rain out of eyes
- Slightly less fluid needed (cooler conditions)

### Wind
- Draft behind other runners when possible
- Adjust effort, not pace — maintain consistent effort level
- Expect slower splits into headwind, faster with tailwind

## Race Week Checklist

### 3 Days Out
- [ ] Begin carb loading
- [ ] Lay out race kit
- [ ] Check race logistics (start time, transport, bag drop)
- [ ] Reduce training to easy 20-30min runs + strides

### Day Before
- [ ] Pin bib to singlet
- [ ] Charge watch
- [ ] Set two alarms
- [ ] Easy 15-20min shakeout run
- [ ] Eat familiar dinner (carb-heavy, low fiber)
- [ ] Prepare race morning bag (gels, watch, shoes, clothes)

### Race Morning
- [ ] Wake 3+ hours before start
- [ ] Eat tested pre-race meal
- [ ] Sip water/sports drink
- [ ] Arrive 60-90min early
- [ ] Light warmup jog 10-15min before start
- [ ] Last bathroom stop
